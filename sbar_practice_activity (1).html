💡 Expert Example for Comparison

🔵 Situation

Expert version: "Mrs. Smith in bed 4 is confused and disoriented, unable to state her name or location post-surgery."

Word count: 17/20 - Clear, specific, immediate concern stated

🟣 Background

Expert version: "Post-op day 1 from gallbladder surgery, was alert and oriented pre-operatively, no prior history of confusion or cognitive impairment documented."

Word count: 22/30 - Relevant context, comparison to baseline

🟢 Assessment

Expert version: "I'm concerned about post-operative delirium or possible adverse medication reaction affecting her mental status."

Word count: 16/20 - Clear clinical thinking, specific concerns

🟠 Recommendation

Expert version: "Please evaluate immediately, consider cognitive assessment and review all current medications for side effects."

Word count: 16/20 - Specific, actionable requests

💡 SBAR Best Practice Tips
  • Be specific: Use exact patient identifiers, room numbers, and measurements
  • Stay objective: Report facts and observations, not interpretations in S and B
  • Think clinically: Show your reasoning in Assessment section
  • Be clear about urgency: Make your timeframe expectations clear in Recommendation
  • Practice brevity: Concise communication reduces errors and improves response
