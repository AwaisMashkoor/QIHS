From Research to Practice

Leadership and Culture in Integrated Care

Findings from doctoral research on leadership in Scottish Integration Joint Boards, and an introduction to the CLI Compass framework

Awais Mashkoor University of the West of Scotland Ayrshire Innovation in Health and Social Care Network 9 December 2025

Why This Matters

Scotland's 2014 Public Bodies (Joint Working) Act created 31 Integration Joint Boards – the most ambitious reform since the NHS was established.

1948 Last reform of this scale
£8.5B Resources at stake
5.5M Scottish population served

"Good governance cannot be engineered through legislation alone"

What I Studied

My PhD examined how context shapes the way leaders actually work within Integration Joint Boards.

11 IJBs Sampled
22 Elite Interviews
85 Documents Analysed

Who I spoke to: Councillors, Chief Officers, NHS Non-Executives
Across: Urban, rural, and mixed contexts – capturing Scotland's diversity

What I Found: Context Matters

Leaders adapt their style depending on circumstances. This isn't weakness – it's reality.

Context Servant Directive Collaborative Situational Democratic Transformational Facilitative Adaptive

The same leader might use transformational approaches in one situation, democratic approaches in budget discussions, directive approaches during crises, and servant approaches when addressing staff concerns.

Financial Pressures Shape Leadership

Fiscal stress forces different responses – from crisis management to creative problem-solving.

Challenges Observed

  • Separate NHS and council budgets create silos
  • Ring-fenced funding limits local flexibility
  • Political leaders often rely heavily on officers for financial detail

Creative Responses

  • Repurposing facilities creatively
  • Collaborative gap-filling between partners
  • Challenging wasteful practices

I witnessed an occupational therapy home visit costing £300 arranged just to provide a long-handled shoehorn. I challenged this – why not a simple phone assessment and postal delivery for £1? This small change freed up our specialists for complex cases while saving £299 per intervention.

— Interviewee E, 2022

Relationships Are Everything

The quality of councillor-officer relationships shapes what leadership approaches become possible.

What Enables Collaboration

  • Trust built over time
  • Mutual respect for different roles
  • Transparency in decision-making
  • Clear communication
  • Acknowledging each other's strengths

What Gets in the Way

  • High turnover disrupts continuity
  • Digital platforms hinder relationship-building
  • New members unfamiliar with system complexity
  • Power imbalances between officers and politicians

The relationship with officers is built on teamwork and trust – something I've carried from my professional life... While there are operational challenges, we focus on the strategic level.

— Leader in the study

Geography Changes the Game

Urban and rural contexts demand fundamentally different leadership approaches.

Urban Contexts

  • Larger boards, more structured discussions
  • Complex demands, multiple competing priorities
  • Formal processes, less personal familiarity
  • → Democratic approaches observed

Rural Contexts

  • Smaller boards – "everybody knows everybody"
  • Service challenges: distance, transport, workforce
  • Community familiarity, pre-existing relationships
  • → Servant approaches observed

You'd learn more from a lunch club visit than a doctor's appointment – people will tell you what really matters, like how bad the bus service is.

— Rural Leader in the study

The Challenge We Face

Despite good intentions and local innovations, systemic issues persist.

Fragmentation

Good practice remains isolated – innovations don't spread across the system

No Coherent Framework

Culture is mentioned everywhere but understood nowhere – no common language or approach

Context Ignored

Leadership development doesn't account for the reality that context shapes what works

We are awash with policies, evidence and best practices. Understanding how we implement positive cultures and leadership is the critical gap.
