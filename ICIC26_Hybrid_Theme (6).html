The problem

A leadership role hiding in plain sight

31
Integration Joint Boards in Scotland, each with elected councillors as voting members
0
Empirical studies on how these elected members exercise leadership within integration governance in Scotland

Elected officials are among the most invested political actors in public organisations. Yet public administration literature contains a significant knowledge gap on the precise role local political leaders play in strategic decision-making within integrated care governance.

The 2014 Public Bodies Act placed councillors alongside NHS non-executives with voting power over health and social care — a governance space where political authority meets professional expertise.

What role do local political leaders play in strategic decision-making — and how does political context shape the leadership they practise?
The Scottish context

Integration Joint Boards: a natural laboratory

Scotland's Public Bodies (Joint Working) Act 2014 created a unique governance structure where councillors and NHS non-executives share authority over integrated health and social care.
