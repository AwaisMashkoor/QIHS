Developing a Growth Mindset

How you think about learning shapes how you learn.

Fixed Mindset

  • "I'm just not good at academic writing"
  • "Some people are naturally smart"
  • "If I struggle, I should give up"
  • "Feedback means I've failed"

Growth Mindset

  • "I can improve my writing with practice"
  • "Effort and strategy build ability"

  • "Feedback helps me develop"

Your brain can grow and change throughout your life. Academic skills are learnable.

Why This Matters for Your Profession

As future health and social care practitioners, academic integrity connects directly to professional practice.

Professional Registration

The SSSC, NMC, and HCPC all require practitioners to be honest, trustworthy, and work within their competence.

Evidence-Based Practice

Good care depends on honest, accurate information. If you learn to cut corners academically, you might do the same in practice.

Service User Safety

People's wellbeing depends on practitioners who genuinely know their stuff‚Äînot those who've faked their way through.

Understanding Plagiarism

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's work or ideas as your own. It includes:

  • Copying text without quotation marks or citation
  • Paraphrasing without acknowledging the source
  • Submitting work done by someone else
  • Using images, diagrams, or data without attribution
  • Self-plagiarism (resubmitting your own previous work)

Consequences

Plagiarism is taken seriously at UWS. Consequences can range from resubmission to failure of the module or programme.

More importantly, it undermines your own learning and professional development.

Evaluating Sources: The TRAAP Test

Before using any source, ask yourself these questions:

T

Timeliness

When was it published? Is it current enough?

R

Relevance

Does it relate to your topic and audience?

A

Authority

Who wrote it? What are their credentials?

A

Accuracy

Is it supported by evidence? Can you verify it?

P

Purpose

Why was it written? To inform, persuade, sell?

Referencing: Giving Credit

Referencing is how you acknowledge where your information comes from. At UWS, we use Cite Them Right Harvard style.

Why Reference?

  • Shows the evidence behind your arguments
  • Allows readers to check your sources
  • Demonstrates your reading and research
  • Avoids plagiarism
  • Builds your academic credibility

Two Parts

1. In-text Citation

Short reference in the body of your work, e.g., (Smith, 2023) or Smith (2023) states...

2. Reference List

Full details at the end of your work so readers can find the source.
